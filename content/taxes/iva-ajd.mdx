<SummaryBox>
New-build properties from a developer are taxed differently from resales. You pay IVA (VAT) at 10% instead of ITP, plus AJD (stamp duty) at 1.5% in the Balearics. On a €600,000 new build, that's €60,000 IVA + €9,000 AJD = €69,000 in purchase taxes. For comparison, ITP on the same resale property would be €57,000.
</SummaryBox>

## IVA vs ITP: which applies to you?

The key distinction:
- **Second-hand property** (resale from a private owner) → ITP applies
- **New-build from a developer** (primera transmisión) → IVA + AJD applies
- **Plot of land** → IVA + AJD applies

## IVA (VAT) on new builds

The standard IVA rate for residential new builds in Spain is **10%**. This is a national rate — the same everywhere in Spain. Land and commercial properties pay the full 21% IVA rate.

IVA is collected by the developer and paid to Hacienda. As the buyer, you simply pay the total price including IVA.

## AJD (Stamp Duty)

AJD (Actos Jurídicos Documentados) is the stamp duty charged on notarised documents. It applies when:
- Buying a new-build property (alongside IVA)
- Refinancing a mortgage
- Creating or modifying a mortgage

In the Balearic Islands, the AJD rate for new residential property is **1.5%** of the purchase price.

<Callout variant="info" title="Mortgage AJD: the law changed in 2019">
Since November 2018, banks pay AJD on new mortgages — not buyers. You still pay AJD on the property purchase itself (as part of the new-build transaction), but you no longer pay AJD on your mortgage separately.
</Callout>

## Example: New build apartment, €500,000

- IVA: €500,000 × 10% = **€50,000**
- AJD: €500,000 × 1.5% = **€7,500**
- **Total taxes: €57,500**

Plus notary fees (typically €1,000–€2,000) and Land Registry fees (€500–€1,000).

## Can IVA be reclaimed?

Not for a private residential purchase. If you are buying through a company or the property is exclusively for business use (e.g. commercial rental), IVA may be recoverable. For personal holiday home purchases, it is not.

## New builds and Modelo 210

Once you own a new-build property, the same Modelo 210 rules apply as for any non-resident property owner. You will owe annual non-resident income tax on the property from the year of purchase.
