<SummaryBox>
Modelo 210 is filed annually by every non-resident property owner in Spain. Tax is calculated on a "deemed rental income" derived from your property's cadastral value — not actual rent. This calculator gives an indicative estimate.
</SummaryBox>

<Callout variant="info" title="This is an estimate only">
The calculator below is for planning purposes. Your actual Modelo 210 may differ depending on your municipality's cadastral revision date, your ownership share, and any partial-year ownership. Always have a gestoría verify your final filing.
</Callout>

## How the tax is calculated

Modelo 210 for a property used personally (not rented out) works as follows:

1. **Deemed rental income** = cadastral value × 1.1% (or 2% if cadastral value has not been revised since 1994)
2. **Tax** = deemed rental income × tax rate (19% for EU/EEA residents, 24% for others)

## What is the cadastral value?

The **valor catastral** is an administrative value assigned to your property by the Spanish authorities. It is always lower than market value — typically 30–70% of it. You can find it on your IBI (local property tax) bill.

## Key deadlines

Modelo 210 must be filed and paid by **31 December** of the year following the tax year. For the 2025 tax year, the deadline is 31 December 2026.
