## What is a gestoría, and why do you need one?

A **gestoría** is a Spanish professional firm that acts as your administrative and tax representative in Spain. It occupies a role that doesn't have a direct equivalent in most other countries — part accountant, part tax agent, part administrative fixer. For non-resident property owners, a reliable gestoría is arguably the most important professional relationship you will have in Spain.

Without one, you are responsible for:
- Filing your own Modelo 210 (non-resident income tax) each year
- Setting up and managing your IBI direct debit
- Obtaining and renewing your NIE
- Navigating Modelo 030 (change of address and census notifications)
- Handling correspondence from Hacienda or ATIB
- Coordinating any property purchase, sale, or inheritance process

With a gestoría, all of this is handled on your behalf, and you are alerted when action or payment is required.

## Why a local Mallorcan gestoría matters

Some non-resident owners use a gestoría or tax agent in their home country who offers Spanish filings as an add-on service. This can work for the simplest cases — a single property, no rental income, no complications. For anything more involved, a Mallorca-based gestoría has advantages:

- **Local knowledge** of Mallorcan municipality systems, ATIB processes, and Balearic-specific rules (including Balearic succession tax reliefs and regional IBI variations)
- **Direct relationships** with the Policía Nacional, ATIB, Registro de la Propiedad, and local notarios
- **On-the-ground presence** for documents that require in-person handling

A home-country adviser working remotely may not know, for example, that a specific Mallorcan municipality has changed its IBI direct debit process, or that ATIB requires a particular form for a specific transaction. A local gestoría deals with these things every day.
